# Day 6 - Transformers, Tokenization, Generative AI, and Model Benchmarking

## Overview

The sixth day of the internship focused on Natural Language Processing (NLP), Transformer architectures, tokenization techniques, Generative AI applications, and benchmarking of Large Language Models (LLMs). The session combined theoretical concepts with practical implementations using modern AI platforms and APIs.

## Learning Objectives

* Understand the fundamentals of Transformers.
* Learn the concept of Word Embeddings.
* Explore Tokenization techniques used in NLP.
* Work with Generative AI models for Text-to-Speech and Text-to-Image tasks.
* Compare the performance of different AI models through benchmarking.

## Topics Covered

### 1. Introduction to Transformers

The session began with an introduction to Transformer architecture and its importance in modern Natural Language Processing.

Topics discussed:

* Sequence modeling
* Self-Attention Mechanism
* Encoder-Decoder Architecture
* Applications of Transformers in NLP and Generative AI

### 2. Word Embeddings

Learned how words can be represented as numerical vectors for machine learning models.

Concepts covered:

* Vector Representation of Words
* Semantic Relationships
* Contextual Understanding
* Importance of Embeddings in NLP

### 3. Tokenization

Explored how text is converted into tokens before being processed by language models.

Topics covered:

* Word Tokenization
* Subword Tokenization
* Sentence Tokenization
* Token IDs and Vocabulary Mapping

### 4. Generative AI Applications

Using a Groq API key, practical implementations of Generative AI applications were performed.

Activities:

* Text-to-Speech Generation
* Text-to-Image Generation
* Understanding API-based AI integrations
* Working with prompt-driven generation workflows

### 5. Benchmarking Language Models

Performed benchmarking experiments to compare the performance of different NLP and Large Language Models.

#### Models Explored

* BERT
* GPT
* Groq Models
* Gemini Models

#### Benchmarking Activities

* Executed predefined prompts across multiple models.
* Compared response quality and performance.
* Analyzed model behavior for different NLP tasks.
* Observed differences between encoder-based and generative models.

## Key Learnings

* Fundamentals of Transformer Architecture.
* Importance of Word Embeddings in NLP.
* Tokenization techniques used by modern language models.
* Working with Generative AI APIs.
* Text-to-Speech and Text-to-Image generation workflows.
* Benchmarking and evaluating AI models.
* Understanding the differences between BERT, GPT, Groq, and Gemini-based models.

## Outcome

Successfully gained practical exposure to modern NLP concepts, Transformer-based architectures, Generative AI applications, and model benchmarking techniques. Developed an understanding of how different AI models perform across various language and generation tasks.
